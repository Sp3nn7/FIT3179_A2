Global Contributions to Terrorism

Figure 1 uses a choropleth map to display each countries percentage contribution towards the global terrorism count in from 2014-2017.

 We can see that the areas that have contributed the most to this count in this 3 year period are countries apart of or surrounding the Middle East, where Iraq, Afghanistan and Pakistan contributed 43% globally in this period.

Yearly and Monthly Trends in Global Attack Occurrences

Figures 2 and 3 highlight both the yearly number of attacks based on the type of attack as well as the monthly breakdown of attacks for the given year respectively.

Throughout the years of 2014-2017, bombings/explosions are the vehicle for most terrorist attacks in this period.

What is interesting to see is that globally, the number of attacks seems constant throughout the individual months of the year. As we use these visualizations to attempt to highlight a trend, we attempt to find a daily trend in figure 4 below.

Global Daily Terrorism Incidents

Figure 4 attempts to highlight a trend based on the specific days in the year/month. Through creating a waffle chart, we can visualize how many days in the month terrorist attacks occur.

Unfortunately, we can see that we do not have a single day through this 3 year period where a terrorist attack hasn’t occurred.
Because of this, we have decided to encode colour with the number of attacks on the given day. By doing this we can attempt to find if any days in particular cause an increase in terror.

Unfortunately, no trend can directly be seen from this waffle chart, where these attacks don’t appear to highlight a pattern based on a specific day in the year.

What is the global death toll resulting from these attacks?

As can be seen from figure 7, this boxplot describes the distribution of fatalities from terrorist attacks from 2014-2017 for specific weapon types. Throughout this period, although there have been countless terrorist attacks (refer to figures 1-5) the fatalities are quite low, where the median number of fatalities is 1 (per weapon categoty).

Which individuals or Groups are being Targeted?

Figure 6 attempts to highlight which groups or people have been targeted more frequently in these attacks from 2014-2017 through a word cloud. This word cloud highlights key words that appear frequently throughout the dataset in order to deduce the frequency of attacks .

 As can be seen the most common targets are unknown, highlighting that these attacks either have no target in mind or that the dataset is limited. We can also see keywords such as Taliban, Extremists and Iraq which agree with Figure 1's depiction of countries with high counts of terrorism.

Who is responsible for the terrorist attacks?

Figure 5 utilizes a pie chart to describe which terrorist organizations are performing attacks based on the weapon type and year.

 Unfortunately, a worrying statistic that is highlighted through this visualisation is that in nearly all years and weapon types, unknown people/organizations are performing the most number of attacks.
